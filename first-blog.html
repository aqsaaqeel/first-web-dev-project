Why is .com the most used domain name

How it started?

.com derives its name from the word commercial because originally it was created to be used by organisations for commercial purposes. It was started in 1985 and administered by United States Department of Defense, now it is under Verisign. It is a top-level domain(TLD). Although .com is one of the first domains, there were other top level domains available since a long time like edu, gov, net, org, etc but .com is the largest top-level domain. Today, roughly 52% of websites use .com which leads us to the question as to why and how has it garnered such popularity.

Why is it more preferred over the others?

When making a website, choosing domains are as important as choosing the website’s name. It can have a lot of effect on a website’s traffic. When users see your website, the only way they can come back is through the URL. Our brains have become wired into thinking that most websites end with .com domain that whenever we are searching for a website, we search for a .com one. It is also speculated that Google SEO tends to rank these websites higher because they are readable, recognisable and trustworthy..

The simple reason for this could be that it is one of the oldest and reliable domains. Seeing a website end with .com may just give off the vibe of authority. We can think of a scenario where a person can not get a .com domain with their preferred URL so they go for a lesser known domain. A problem that arises is that when someone searches for that person’s website they are bound to search for their website name with a .com suffix and it ends up leading them to some other website. Thereby, decreasing this person’s website traffic.

We know that most websites are used for commercial purposes so a .com domain sounds appropriate for that. There are country based domain names available but to someone from a different country it may not sound inclusive. At the end of the day, a person can choose a domain according to their own needs. For example, a government scholarship portal will sound more appropriate with that country’s domain name. But it is needless to say that for general purposes, .com definitely has an upper hand over its peers.
